● The Problem

Date management in UK grocery
is still done by hand.

Every day, colleagues walk Market Street and every fresh aisle, scanning every line, hoping to catch what's about to expire. It's slow, imperfect, and quietly erodes the fresh margin, and the customer trust, you're working to win back.

Hours of colleague time, every week
Colleagues spend hours manually checking dates, walking every aisle, touching every product. Time that should go to customers and Market Street, not date-code admin.
Short-dated stock reaches the shelf
Manual checks are inconsistent. Products slip through. An out-of-date item on the shelf is a food-safety risk and a direct hit to the brand customers are meant to choose, not just use.
Markdowns too late or too early
Without real-time tracking, you reduce-to-clear products that still hold full margin, or miss the window entirely and write them off as waste at full cost.
HQ blind to store-level risk
No real-time view across 497 supermarkets. Waste data arrives weeks late. You're reacting, not preventing, and you can't see which stores need help today.
