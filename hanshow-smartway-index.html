● The Problem

Date management in US grocery
is still done by hand.

Every week, teams walk every aisle, scan every SKU, hoping to catch what's about to expire. It's slow, imperfect, and costs you money every single day.

Hours of labor, every week
Associates spend hours manually checking expiration dates, walking every aisle, touching every product. Highest-labor, lowest-signal task in the store.
Expired stock reaches customers
Manual checks are inconsistent. Products slip through. Expired items on the shelf are a compliance risk, a trust problem, and a lawsuit waiting to happen.
Markdowns too late or too early
Without real-time tracking, you discount products that still have margin left, or miss the window entirely and write them off at full cost.
HQ blind to store-level risk
No real-time view across stores. Shrink data arrives weeks late. You're reacting to problems, not preventing them, and you can't benchmark which stores are struggling.
